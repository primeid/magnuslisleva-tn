Software is usually described as if it were a building: solid, stable, designed from above.

But much of the interesting work feels ecological instead. Systems grow, compete for resources,
decay, and adapt around constraints. That does not make engineering less rigorous. It means rigor
has to include observation, maintenance, and timing.

If a blog is going to hold code-adjacent writing and media work at the same time, that ecological
view is useful. It allows the archive to become a living structure rather than a shelf.
